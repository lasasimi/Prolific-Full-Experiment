Consent for participation

Dear participant,

We would like to invite you to participate in a research study conducted by the researchers from the Wrocław University of Science and Technology and the Complexity Science Hub Vienna. Your participation in this study is entirely voluntary. Please read the information below and ask any questions you may have before deciding whether or not to participate. You may refuse to participate at any time without giving a reason, and you can also withdraw your consent after the study has begun. However, payment is contingent upon completing the session through the end. There will be no negative consequences if you choose not to participate or if you decide to withdraw early.

Please confirm that you wish to participate in this study only if:
  • you have fully understood the type and procedure of the study,
  • you are willing to give your consent to participate, and
  • you are aware of your rights as a participant in this study.
What is the purpose of this study?

The purpose of this study is to investigate how people form opinions in groups. Results from this study will help us understand collective phenomena such as polarization and consensus. You have been invited to participate in this study because you are at least 18 years of age.

What is the procedure of the study?

If you volunteer to participate in this study, you will be asked for your opinions about what to do in various dilemmas that could occur among neighbors in a village. The study will last approximately 20 minutes.

What are the benefits of participating in the study?

Your participation will help us understand how people form opinions in groups. You will also be compensated for the duration of the study according to standard hourly rates.

What are the possible risks for taking in this study? Could participants experience any discomfort or other side effects?

There are no foreseeable risks and we expect no discomfort, or any other side effects, from your participation in the study.

Does participating in the study have any other effects on participants’ lifestyle? What are the obligations resulting from participating?

Study participation is not expected to have any other impacts on your lifestyle. There are no future obligations associated with participating in the study.

What should participants do if they experience symptoms of complaints, unwanted side effects and/or injuries?

If you have any negative experiences during the study, please contact the study coordinator Dr. Gabriela Juncosa from the Complexity Science Hub Vienna, who you can contact at juncosa@csh.ac.at.

In what cases is it necessary that participants withdraw from the study early?

Your participation in this research study is voluntary. You may refuse to participate without penalty at any time. If you decide to participate, you are free to stop at any time without penalty by just stopping and/or telling the investigator. If you complete the study but later decide that you want your data removed, please contact the study coordinator Dr. Gabriela Juncosa at juncosa@csh.ac.at.

How will the data collected in this study be used?

The personal data collected from you is the minimum relevant to the scope of the present study, and it will only be used for the specific, explicit and legitimate purposes for which they are obtained. Data on your actions during your participation will also be collected and used for research purposes. Once your data has been fully anonymized and cannot be linked back to you in any way, it will be analyzed and placed in an Open Data Repository to be shared with other researchers. This will be done solely for research purposes and not for commercial use, in compliance with EU policy. Aggregated data may be shared to support reproducibility of published results. Personal data will not be used for purposes that are incompatible with the purposes for which the data were collected. Personal data will be stored on our servers until you lodge an objection or withdraw your consent. The data will not be transferred outside the EU. The personal data collected is not subject to automated decision-making, and not subject to profiling.

What are your rights regarding the processing of your personal data?

You have the right to object to the processing of your data for marketing purposes or purposes related to conducting quality and satisfaction surveys – as we are processing your data on the basis of legitimate interest; the right to object to the processing of your data on grounds of special circumstances – in cases where we process your data on the basis of our legitimate interest for purposes other than those set out in the item above, the right to request that your personal data be rectified; the right to request the erasure of your personal data, only if we are not obliged by law to process it, the rights to request that the processing of your personal data should be restricted; the right to have your data transferred, i.e. the right to receive your personal data from us – in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable computer format. You may transfer this data to another data controller on your own or request that we transfer your data to another controller. However, we will only do this if such a transfer is feasible. Finally, you have the right to access your personal data. In order to exercise these rights, please contact the study coordinator Dr. Gabriela Juncosa at juncosa@csh.ac.at.

Will there be any costs for the participants? Will they receive reimbursement or remuneration?

There is no cost for taking part in the study. You will be compensated for the duration of the study according to the standard hourly rate. The study consists of three phases, where the first two are completed individually, with no interaction with others. You will receive a base payment of USD 1.40 only if you fully complete both Phase 1 and Phase 2. An additional bonus is available if you if you agree to participate in a group discussion (Phase 3).

Possibility to discuss further questions

If you have any further questions about the study, or about your rights as a participant, we encourage you to contact the study coordinator Dr. Gabriela Juncosa at juncosa@csh.ac.at.

If I want to withdraw from the study, I can do so at any time by contacting the study coordinator Dr. Gabriela Juncosa at juncosa@csh.ac.at.
